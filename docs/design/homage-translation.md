# Otherside Defense — Translating X-COM to the Hell Dimension

Companion to `docs/research/xcom-ufo-defense-reference.md`. That doc says how
the 1994 game works; this one proposes how each system re-skins (or mutates)
when the invaders are demonic forces from a hell dimension — the **Otherside**
— instead of aliens from space. Names are working titles, all open to change.

## Premise

Reality is thinning. Rifts to the Otherside tear open across the globe, and
demons pour through — first probing, then harvesting, then corrupting nations
from within. The world's governments secretly charter **the Order** (working
name) to detect rifts, banish incursions, and reverse-engineer the occult.

The nice thing about the demonic frame: X-COM's mechanics barely have to bend.
Fear, corruption, possession, and forbidden knowledge are already the systems
Gollop built — they were just wearing rubber alien masks.

## The Bestiary, Translated

Keep X-COM's structure: a few breeds, each with a rank ladder and one terror
unit that breaks the rules. Working roster:

| X-COM original | Otherside equivalent | Twist |
|---|---|---|
| Sectoid | **Imp** | weak swarm grunt; imp Overseers get possession powers |
| Floater | **Gargoyle** | flying harasser, perches on rooftops |
| Snakeman | **Hellspawn brute** | slow, armored line demon |
| Muton | **War-fiend** | elite legionnaire of hell |
| Ethereal | **Prince of the Otherside** | frail robed horror, pure possession/temptation |
| Chryssalid | **The Taker** (name TBD) | melee one-hit horror that turns victims — the possessed stand up and birth another. Do not soften this. |
| Zombie | **Husk / the Taken** | every one is a Taker in waiting |
| Cyberdisc | **Bone-engine** | flying construct, explodes on death |
| Reaper | **Hellhound** | huge melee beast, small-arms-resistant hide |
| Sectopod | **Iron idol** | walking altar-tank, resistant to blessed weapons |
| Celatid / Silacoid | **Bile-wisp / Cinderling** | acid spitter / crawling fire-starter |

Rank ladders: Drone → Soldier → Overseer → Herald → **Prince** (live Prince
capture unlocks the endgame, mirroring Leader/Commander).

## Where We Deliberately Diverge (proposals, not decisions)

1. **Dread as a first-class system.** X-COM's morale was half-hidden. We
   surface it: a visible Dread meter per soldier, demons that feed on it, and
   equipment/rituals that manage it. Bravery growth ("surviving the pit") stays.
2. **Corruption trade-offs.** Using hellfire weapons, unholy foci, or reading
   deep Codex entries could tick a per-soldier (or organization-wide)
   Corruption track — power now, consequences later. This replaces nothing in
   X-COM; it's our signature addition. Needs careful tuning to avoid punishing
   engagement.
3. **Rifts don't have to fly.** Interception can become a timing/priority game
   (hit the rift while it's unstable) rather than a literal dogfight — or keep
   aerial contests for winged hosts and tribute caravans. Decide in prototype.
4. **Night is the default.** Demons come in darkness; daylight missions are the
   rare relief. Flares/consecrated light become core kit, inverting X-COM's
   day/night rhythm.
5. **Keep the factory exploit, tamed.** Selling blessed arms to national
   militaries is thematic income — cap demand rather than removing it.

## What We Keep Untouched (the pillars)

- Two interlocking layers; tactical results rewrite the strategic game.
- Permadeath + learn-by-doing stats. No classes. Biography is the build.
- Simulation-first tactics: real projectiles, destructible terrain, per-facing
  armor, TU economy with emergent reaction fire.
- Scarcity (brimstone, live captures) that money can't substitute.
- The readable escalation director generating incursion waves with intent.
- Losing slowly: funding collapse and cult infiltration as the long defeat.
